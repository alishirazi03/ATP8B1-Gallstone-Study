## 1. Study Design

### Study Type
**Case-Control Study**

### Study Population
- **Total participants:** 104
- **Cases:** 75 gallstone patients
- **Controls:** 29 healthy individuals

### Inclusion Criteria

**For Cases:**
- Confirmed diagnosis of gallstone disease by:
  - Ultrasonography, OR
  - Surgical confirmation (cholecystectomy)
- Age: 18-70 years
- Pakistani nationality
- Willing to provide informed consent

**For Controls:**
- No history of gallstone disease
- No symptoms of biliary disease
- Age-matched to cases (±5 years)
- Pakistani nationality
- Willing to provide informed consent

### Exclusion Criteria

**For All Participants:**
- Pregnancy
- Known liver disease (cirrhosis, hepatitis B/C)
- Malignancy
- Hemolytic anemia
- Use of medications affecting bile metabolism
- Inability to provide informed consent
- Age <18 or >70 years

### Sample Size Calculation

Sample size determined based on:
- Expected minor allele frequency: 10-20% (from literature estimates)
- Power: 80%
- Significance level (α): 0.05
- Odds ratio to detect: ≥2.0
- Calculated minimum sample: 60 cases, 30 controls

**Final recruitment:** 75 cases, 29 controls (exceeded minimum requirements)

## 3. Sample Collection

### Blood Sample Collection

**Volume:** 3-5 mL peripheral blood

**Collection Tube:** EDTA vacutainer (purple/lavender cap)
- EDTA prevents coagulation
- Preserves DNA integrity

**Collection Procedure:**
1. Venipuncture performed by trained phlebotomist
2. Site cleaned with 70% isopropyl alcohol
3. Blood drawn into EDTA tube
4. Tube inverted gently 8-10 times to mix with anticoagulant
5. Sample labeled with unique ID immediately

**Storage:**
- **Short-term:** 4°C for up to 7 days before DNA extraction
- **Long-term:** -20°C for samples not immediately processed

**Demographic Data Collection:**
At time of sample collection, recorded:
- Age
- Gender
- Ethnicity
- Geographic region
- Clinical diagnosis (for cases)
- Comorbidities
- Family history of gallstone disease

## 4. DNA Extraction

### Method: Organic Extraction (Phenol-Chloroform-Isoamyl Alcohol)

**Principle:** 
Protein denaturation and separation using organic solvents, followed by DNA precipitation with ethanol.

### Reagents Required

| Reagent | Composition | Purpose |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| RBC Lysis Buffer | NH₄Cl, KHCO₃, EDTA | Remove red blood cells |
| WBC Lysis Buffer | Tris-HCl, EDTA, SDS | Lyse white blood cells |
| Proteinase K | 20 mg/mL in water | Digest proteins |
| Phenol:Chloroform:Isoamyl alcohol | 25:24:1 ratio | Separate proteins from DNA |
| Chloroform:Isoamyl alcohol | 24:1 ratio | Remove phenol residue |
| 100% Ethanol (cold) | -20°C | Precipitate DNA |
| 70% Ethanol (cold) | -20°C | Wash DNA pellet |
| TE Buffer | 10 mM Tris-HCl, 1 mM EDTA, pH 8.0 | Dissolve and store DNA |

### Detailed Protocol

#### Step 1: Red Blood Cell (RBC) Lysis
1. Transfer 300 μL whole blood to 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tube
2. Add 900 μL RBC lysis buffer (1:3 ratio)
3. Mix gently by inversion (10 times)
4. Incubate at room temperature for 10 minutes
5. Centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 2 minutes
6. Discard supernatant carefully (contains RBCs)
7. Repeat if pellet appears red

**Result:** White pellet of white blood cells (WBCs)

#### Step 2: White Blood Cell (WBC) Lysis
1. Add 600 μL WBC lysis buffer to pellet
2. Vortex to resuspend pellet completely
3. Add 10 μL Proteinase K (20 mg/mL)
4. Mix gently
5. Incubate at 55°C for 2-3 hours (or overnight at 37°C)
   - Mix occasionally by gentle inversion

**Result:** Clear, viscous lysate

#### Step 3: Protein Removal (Organic Extraction)

**First Extraction:**
1. Add equal volume (600 μL) phenol:chloroform:isoamyl alcohol
2. Mix vigorously by vortexing for 30 seconds
3. Centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 10 minutes
4. **Observe three layers:**
   - Top (aqueous): DNA
   - Middle (interface): denatured proteins
   - Bottom (organic): phenol-chloroform
5. Carefully transfer upper aqueous layer to new tube
   - Use pipette, avoid touching interface
   - Transfer ~500-550 μL

**Second Extraction:**
1. Add equal volume chloroform:isoamyl alcohol to aqueous phase
2. Mix by vortexing for 30 seconds
3. Centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 10 minutes
4. Transfer upper aqueous layer to new tube
   - Should be clear, not cloudy

**Result:** Purified DNA in aqueous solution

#### Step 4: DNA Precipitation
1. Add 2 volumes (1000-1100 μL) of ice-cold 100% ethanol
2. Add 1/10 volume (50-60 μL) of 3M sodium acetate (pH 5.2)
3. Mix gently by inversion (do NOT vortex)
4. Incubate at -20°C for 30 minutes (or overnight)
5. Centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 15 minutes at 4°C
6. Discard supernatant carefully
   - DNA pellet may be visible (white)

#### Step 5: DNA Washing
1. Add 500 μL ice-cold 70% ethanol
2. Centrifuge at 12,000 rpm for 5 minutes
3. Discard supernatant
4. Repeat wash once more
5. Air dry pellet for 10-15 minutes
   - Leave tube open in laminar flow hood
   - Do NOT over-dry (DNA becomes difficult to dissolve)

#### Step 6: DNA Resuspension
1. Add 50-100 μL TE buffer (pH 8.0)
2. Incubate at 55°C for 15 minutes to dissolve
3. Store at 4°C (short-term) or -20°C (long-term)

### Expected Yield
- **Typical yield:** 20-50 μg DNA from 300 μL blood
- **Concentration:** 200-500 ng/μL
- **Volume:** 50-100 μL

### Quality Control
- **Purity check:** 260/280 ratio should be 1.8-2.0
- **Visual check:** Run 2 μL on 0.8% agarose gel
  - Should see high molecular weight band (>10 kb)
  - No smearing indicates intact DNA

## 5. DNA Quantification and Quality Assessment

### Method: Agarose Gel Electrophoresis

**Purpose:** 
- Assess DNA integrity
- Estimate DNA concentration visually
- Confirm successful extraction

### 0.8% Agarose Gel Preparation

**Materials:**
- Agarose powder
- 1X TBE buffer (Tris-Borate-EDTA)
- Ethidium bromide (10 mg/mL) OR SYBR Safe

**Procedure:**
1. Weigh 0.8 g agarose powder
2. Add 100 mL 1X TBE buffer in flask
3. Microwave in 30-second intervals until fully dissolved
   - Swirl between intervals
   - Be careful of boiling over
4. Cool to ~60°C (comfortable to touch)
5. Add 5 μL ethidium bromide (FINAL: 0.5 μg/mL)
   - ⚠️ Handle with gloves (mutagenic)
6. Pour into gel tray with comb
7. Allow to solidify (30-40 minutes)

### Sample Loading
1. Mix 2 μL DNA + 1 μL 6X loading dye
2. Load into wells
3. Load DNA ladder (λ DNA or high MW marker) in first lane

### Electrophoresis Conditions
- **Buffer:** 1X TBE
- **Voltage:** 80-100V
- **Time:** 30-45 minutes
- **Stop when:** Dye front reaches 2/3 of gel

### Visualization
- **UV transilluminator** at 302 nm
- Document with gel documentation system
- Assess:
  - Presence of high molecular weight band
  - No smearing (indicates degradation)
  - Estimate concentration by comparing to ladder

### DNA Concentration Estimation
Visual comparison to known standards:
- Faint band: ~50 ng/μL
- Moderate band: ~100-200 ng/μL
- Strong band: >300 ng/μL

**For PCR:** Dilute to working concentration of ~50-100 ng/μL

## 6. Primer Design

### Target SNP Information

**SNP ID:** rs766737165  
**Location:** ATP8B1 gene, exon 19  
**Chromosome:** 18  
**Reference Position:** NC_000018.10  
**Nucleotide Change:** T>A (Wild-type T, Variant A)

### Design Strategy: TETRA-ARMS PCR

**Principle:** Four primers in single reaction
- 2 outer primers: amplify control fragment (both alleles)
- 2 inner primers: allele-specific (differentiate T vs A)

### Primer Design Criteria

**General Requirements:**
- Length: 18-30 bp
- Tm: 55-62°C (within 3°C of each other)
- GC content: 40-60%
- No secondary structures (hairpins, dimers)
- No runs of identical nucleotides (>4 in a row)

**Allele-Specific Primers (Inner):**
- 3' end MUST match SNP nucleotide exactly
- Penultimate mismatch optional (increases specificity)
- Tm slightly higher than outer primers

### Designed Primer Set

| Primer | Sequence (5'→3') | Length | Tm (°C) | Product |
|--------|------------------|--------|---------|---------|
| **FO** (Forward Outer) | ACTTCCTAGAAAACATTCCTTACC | 24 bp | 56.2°C | 486 bp |
| **RO** (Reverse Outer) | ACAATGTGACTCTGAAAGTTAAAA | 24 bp | 54.8°C | 486 bp |
| **FI** (Forward Inner, T) | GTTTCTTTCTTCTGGAGATTTCTGACAT | 28 bp | 58.1°C | 321 bp |
| **RI** (Reverse Inner, A) | CCTGTTGTAAACAGAGGGATTTTTTAAA | 28 bp | 57.9°C | 237 bp |

### Expected Amplicon Sizes

| Genotype | Bands Observed | Sizes |
|----------|----------------|-------|
| **TT** (wild-type) | 2 bands | 486 bp + 321 bp |
| **TA** (heterozygous) | 3 bands | 486 bp + 321 bp + 237 bp |
| **AA** (variant) | 2 bands | 486 bp + 237 bp |

**Control band (486 bp):** Present in ALL genotypes (internal PCR control)

### Primer Validation

**BLAST Analysis:**
- Checked against human genome (NCBI BLAST)
- Confirmed specificity to ATP8B1 locus
- No predicted off-target amplification
- No significant homology to other genes

**Secondary Structure Check:**
- No hairpins (ΔG > -3 kcal/mol)
- No self-dimers
- No hetero-dimers between primers
- Checked using OligoAnalyzer (IDT)

### Primer Synthesis
- **Supplier:** [Commercial DNA synthesis company]
- **Purification:** Desalted (standard)
- **Scale:** 25 nmol
- **Quality control:** Mass spectrometry verified

### Primer Preparation

**Stock Solution (100 μM):**
1. Spin down lyophilized primers
2. Add appropriate volume nuclease-free water
   - Volume = nmol × 10 μL (for 100 μM)
3. Vortex and centrifuge briefly
4. Store at -20°C

**Working Solution (10 μM):**
1. Dilute stock 1:10 in nuclease-free water
2. Aliquot to avoid freeze-thaw cycles
3. Store at -20°C

## 7. PCR Amplification

### TETRA-ARMS PCR Reaction Setup

**Reaction Volume:** 25 μL per sample

### Master Mix Composition

| Component | Stock Conc. | Volume (μL) | Final Conc. |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| 10X PCR Buffer | 10X | 2.5 | 1X |
| MgCl₂ | 25 mM | 2.0 | 2.0 mM |
| dNTP mix | 10 mM each | 0.5 | 200 μM each |
| FO primer | 10 μM | 0.5 | 0.2 μM |
| RO primer | 10 μM | 0.5 | 0.2 μM |
| FI primer | 10 μM | 1.0 | 0.4 μM |
| RI primer | 10 μM | 1.0 | 0.4 μM |
| Taq polymerase | 5 U/μL | 0.2 | 1 U |
| Template DNA | 50 ng/μL | 2.0 | 100 ng total |
| Nuclease-free H₂O | - | 14.8 | - |
| **Total** | | **25.0** | |

**Note:** Inner primers (FI, RI) at 2× concentration of outer primers for optimal allele-specific amplification

### Reaction Preparation

**Setup (on ice):**
1. Calculate number of reactions needed
   - Include positive and negative controls
   - Add 10% extra for pipetting error
2. Prepare master mix (all components except DNA)
3. Aliquot 23 μL master mix per tube
4. Add 2 μL template DNA
5. Mix gently, centrifuge briefly
6. Place in thermal cycler

**Controls:**
- **Positive control:** Known DNA sample (confirms PCR works)
- **Negative control (NTC):** No template DNA (detects contamination)

### Thermal Cycling Conditions

**Program:**

| Step | Temperature | Time | Cycles |
|------|-------------|------|--------|
| **Initial Denaturation** | 95°C | 5 min | 1× |
| **Denaturation** | 95°C | 30 sec | |
| **Annealing** | 58°C | 30 sec | 35× |
| **Extension** | 72°C | 45 sec | |
| **Final Extension** | 72°C | 7 min | 1× |
| **Hold** | 4°C | ∞ | - |

**Total Runtime:** ~2 hours 30 minutes

### Optimization Notes

**If poor amplification:**
- Increase template DNA (up to 200 ng)
- Adjust MgCl₂ (try 1.5-3.0 mM range)
- Optimize annealing temperature (gradient PCR: 55-62°C)
- Increase cycle number (up to 40 cycles)

**If non-specific bands:**
- Increase annealing temperature
- Decrease template DNA amount
- Use hot-start Taq polymerase
- Reduce cycle number

## 8. Gel Electrophoresis

### 2% Agarose Gel Preparation

**For PCR Products:**
Higher percentage gel (2%) provides better resolution of fragments 200-500 bp

**Reagents:**
| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Agarose powder | 2.0 g |
| 1X TBE buffer | 100 mL |
| Ethidium bromide | 5 μL (or SYBR Safe) |

**Procedure:**
1. Mix agarose + TBE buffer in flask
2. Microwave until fully dissolved (~2 minutes)
   - Stop and swirl every 30 seconds
3. Cool to ~60°C
4. Add DNA stain (ethidium bromide or SYBR Safe)
5. Pour into gel tray (10 cm × 7 cm)
6. Insert comb (20-well comb)
7. Allow to solidify (30-40 minutes)

### Sample Loading

**Preparation:**
1. Mix 5 μL PCR product + 1 μL 6X loading dye
2. Load immediately into wells

**Loading Order:**
- Lane 1: 100 bp DNA ladder (5 μL)
- Lanes 2-21: PCR samples (6 μL each)
- Include positive and negative controls

### Electrophoresis Conditions

**Parameters:**
- **Buffer:** 1X TBE (fill tank to cover gel)
- **Voltage:** 100V (constant voltage)
- **Current:** Not to exceed 400 mA
- **Time:** 45-60 minutes
- **Stop condition:** Loading dye reaches 2/3 down gel

**Visual Check:**
- Blue dye (bromophenol blue) migrates with ~300 bp fragments
- Yellow dye (xylene cyanol) migrates with ~3000 bp fragments

### DNA Ladder
**100 bp DNA Ladder:**
- Range: 100-1500 bp
- Strong band at 500 bp (reference)
- Use to determine fragment sizes

### Visualization and Documentation

**UV Transillumination:**
- **Wavelength:** 302 nm
- **Safety:** Use face shield, avoid direct UV exposure
- **Exposure time:** Minimize to prevent DNA damage

**Gel Documentation:**
- Capture image with gel doc system
- Settings:
  - Auto-exposure OR manual exposure ~0.5-1.0 seconds
  - Save as high-resolution TIFF or JPEG
  - Label with date, gel number, sample IDs

**Band Analysis:**
- Identify control band (486 bp)
- Identify allele-specific bands (321 bp, 237 bp)
- Score genotypes based on banding pattern

## 9. Genotyping and Scoring

### Scoring Criteria

**Visual Assessment:**

**TT Genotype (Wild-type):**
- ✅ Control band: 486 bp (PRESENT)
- ✅ T-allele band: 321 bp (PRESENT)
- ❌ A-allele band: 237 bp (ABSENT)

**TA Genotype (Heterozygous):**
- ✅ Control band: 486 bp (PRESENT)
- ✅ T-allele band: 321 bp (PRESENT)
- ✅ A-allele band: 237 bp (PRESENT)

**AA Genotype (Homozygous variant):**
- ✅ Control band: 486 bp (PRESENT)
- ❌ T-allele band: 321 bp (ABSENT)
- ✅ A-allele band: 237 bp (PRESENT)

**Failed Sample:**
- ❌ Control band: 486 bp (ABSENT)
- Indicates PCR failure
- Repeat sample

### Quality Control Checks

**Internal Control:**
- 486 bp band MUST be present in all samples
- If absent → PCR failure, repeat

**Positive Control:**
- Known genotype sample
- Should show expected banding pattern
- Confirms reagents and protocol working

**Negative Control (NTC):**
- Should show NO bands
- If bands present → contamination detected

### Data Recording

**Genotyping Results Sheet:**

| Sample ID | 486 bp | 321 bp | 237 bp | Genotype | Quality | Notes |
|-----------|--------|--------|--------|----------|---------|-------|
| CASE001 | + | + | - | TT | Pass | Clear bands |
| CASE002 | + | + | - | TT | Pass | Clear bands |
| CTRL001 | + | + | - | TT | Pass | Clear bands |
| NTC | - | - | - | - | Pass | Clean negative |

**Legend:**
- **+** = Band present
- **-** = Band absent
- **±** = Faint/ambiguous (requires repeat)

### Ambiguous Results

**If bands are faint or unclear:**
1. Repeat PCR with fresh template
2. Increase DNA template amount
3. Run gel for longer time (better resolution)
4. Use fresh reagents
5. Consider Sanger sequencing for confirmation

## 10. Statistical Analysis

### Data Management

**Database:**
- Microsoft Excel spreadsheet
- Columns: Sample ID, Group (Case/Control), Demographics, Genotype
- Double-entry verification for accuracy

### Descriptive Statistics

**Demographics:**
- Age: Mean ± SD, range
- Gender: Frequency and percentage
- Ethnicity: Frequency distribution
- Region: Geographic distribution

**Genotype Frequencies:**
- Count and percentage for each genotype (TT, TA, AA)
- Calculated separately for cases and controls

**Allele Frequencies:**
- T allele frequency = (2×TT + TA) / (2×total)
- A allele frequency = (2×AA + TA) / (2×total)

### Statistical Tests (If Applicable)

**Hardy-Weinberg Equilibrium:**
- Chi-square goodness-of-fit test
- Check if genotype frequencies match expected under HWE
- Separately for cases and controls

**Association Analysis:**
- **Chi-square test:** Compare genotype frequencies between cases and controls
- **Fisher's exact test:** If expected cell counts <5
- **Odds ratio (OR):** Measure of association
- **95% Confidence interval:** Precision of OR estimate
- **P-value:** Statistical significance (α = 0.05)

**Software:**
- GraphPad Prism OR
- SPSS OR
- R statistical software

### Results Interpretation

**In this study:**
- No statistical analysis performed (monomorphic locus)
- All participants showed TT genotype
- No variation to test for association
- Descriptive statistics only

## Summary of Workflow

```

        ↓
DNA Extraction (Phenol-Chloroform method)
        ↓

        ↓

        ↓
Results and Conclusions
```

- DNA extraction: 4-5 hours
- PCR setup and amplification: 3 hours
- Gel electrophoresis: 1.5 hours
- **Total:** ~8-10 hours (can process 10-20 samples in parallel)
